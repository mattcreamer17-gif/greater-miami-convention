The frame

Most problems that look like AI problems are not.

A useful working number across the engagements we run:

  • 60% belongs in ordinary code and a database.
  • 30% belongs in rule-based logic.
  • 10% is a real AI problem.

The Find Your Miami system already has a clean hierarchy, five campaign personas, and neighborhood-level content. That hierarchy is a context architecture. Right now it lives in human heads and PDFs.

Where the leverage sits

The first and highest-leverage move is to make the brand system machine-readable in a way partners, agencies, and internal teams can all draw from, with audit trails.

  • Brief to draft to approved time compresses.
  • Attribution becomes tractable because creative variants carry persona and pillar as structured metadata.
  • Planner responses stop being rebuilt from scratch.

A relevant case

Simple Lyfe Stays.

Simple Lyfe Stays operates 46 short-term-rental properties across a distributed partner network. The shape of the problem is familiar: many units, many partners, many briefs, one finite team.

We shipped a scoped orchestration layer over the existing stack rather than replacing any tool. The point was operating leverage without a platform migration, because platform migrations inside public-sector-adjacent organizations do not survive the political cycle.

The method behind the method

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Submitted to ACM TiiS. Open source on GitHub. The idea: agent context organized as a layered filesystem (identity through working artifacts) with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains.
